I headed up to the local clubhouse to help run the ARRL November Sweepstakes, and it turned into one of my favorite radio days so far. Operating a real contest station with experienced ops beside you is the fastest way to learn that I've found.

I mostly logged and ran short stints at the mic under the watch of the club's seasoned contesters. The pace is relentless — exchange, log, next — but there's a rhythm to it, and by the afternoon I was making contacts without freezing up.

Even as a newer operator there was a real spot for me. That's the thing about club operating events: you can contribute at whatever level you're at and still come away having learned a ton.
