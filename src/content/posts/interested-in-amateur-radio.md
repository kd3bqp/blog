I've been a licensed Amateur Radio operator since July of 2024, and while that isn't a long time, it's been long enough to get completely hooked. If you've ever wondered whether the hobby is worth getting into, here's my honest pitch.

You don't need an expensive station to start. My first real radio was a handheld, and most of my early contacts were on local 2m repeaters and the club net. The barrier to entry is mostly the license exam — and that's very passable with a few weeks of study.

What kept me here wasn't the gear, it was the people: the folks who answered my nervous first call, the club members who waved me over, the operators I've only ever met as a voice on the other end. If any of that sounds appealing, find your local club and go say hi.
