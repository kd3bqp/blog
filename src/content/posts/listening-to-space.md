One of my favorite radio activities doesn't involve transmitting at all — it's listening to satellites. There's something a little magical about pulling a voice or a digital beacon out of a box that's screaming by overhead at 17,000 mph.

With a cheap handheld and a homebrew antenna you can hear the FM amateur satellites on a good pass. I track the pass times, point the antenna, and walk the dial to follow the Doppler shift as the bird comes over the horizon and fades out the other side.

It's a great reminder that the hobby reaches a lot further than the local repeater. Next on my list: actually working a contact through one, not just listening.
